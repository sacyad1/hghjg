What is Water Pollution?

Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies such as rivers, lakes, oceans, and groundwater, usually caused by human activities. This contamination can come from various sources like industrial waste, agricultural runoff, plastic waste, and untreated sewage.

Causes of Water Pollution

  • Industrial discharge of chemicals
  • Agricultural runoff containing pesticides and fertilizers
  • Oil spills and plastic waste
  • Untreated sewage and wastewater
  • Deforestation leading to soil erosion

Effects of Water Pollution

Water pollution has a wide range of harmful effects, including:

  • Health risks like waterborne diseases
  • Destruction of aquatic life
  • Pollution of drinking water resources
  • Disruption of ecosystems
  • Economic losses in fishing and tourism industries

Solutions to Combat Water Pollution

To reduce and prevent water pollution, we can adopt several measures:

  • Proper waste disposal and recycling
  • Reducing chemical and plastic waste
  • Using eco-friendly farming practices
  • Implementing better sewage treatment methods
  • Planting trees to prevent soil erosion
